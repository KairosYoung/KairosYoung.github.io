RESEARCH EXPERIENCE

[1] STEM Education Based On Agricultural Civilization |PI (2020-2022)

 Funded by China Zigen Rural Education Foundation & Faculty of Education, Southwest University.  
Awarded as Sustainable Development Innovation Project for Rural Aesthetic Education by China Zigen Rural Education Foundation; Silver Award at the National Innovation and Entrepreneurship Competition, Chongqing Region

Supervisor：Dr. Xiaowei Tang  & Dr. Xueyin Li

Role: Developed the STEM curriculum (12 class sessions; Hand-cranked grain sifter windmill, a classic agricultural tool) & Conducted the teaching experiments in urban and rural schools (three rounds); designed the test and interview; collected and analyzed the data; writing papers with interdisciplinary team.

Summary: Dream to Practice. Fail but Productive. From Sympathy to See and Care.
Understand more about science education(such as everyday experience & significance of mechanism reasoning ) and rural students ( risks of overemphasizing cultural response; balance the interest and daily life)

Reflection: Why are STEM programs aimed at rural areas more successful in urban settings? When the principal of starting from everyday experience does not work? Rethinking the Tension between Western science and Chinese Traditional Culture.

[2] How we engage with science in daily life? The Empirical Evidence and Insights on the "Everyday Practice" Shift in Science Education |PI (2022-2023)

Funded by Faculty of Education, East China Normal University. Evaluated as Outstanding.

Supervisor：Dr. Sihan Xiao

Purpose: I want to understand why we need to teach science, as well as what we have learned and how we have learned science, before I decide to commit to my career for science education.

Reflection: Rethink the Affective and Cultural aspects of Competent Outsider. Hope to further explore the relationship between the knowledge, functional and cultural goal of science education.

[3] Exploration on the Discourse of “Teachers Must Be Strict"? — Disciplinary Practices in the Educational Risk|PI (2023-2024)

Funded by Faculty of Education, East China Normal University.

Purpose: I am confused and curious about why so many teachers in China tell me that “we must be strict, even if it’s just pretended”.

Reflection: I find the unreliable reasoning and the ethical risks in this discourse. Discipline is not meant to prevent people, but to educate them.

[4] Curriculum Investigation and Redesign for “Hospital School” | Graduate Research Assistant (2024)

Funded by New Sunshine Charity Foundation (WHO Partnership, Member of International Anti-Cancer Alliance)

PI: Dr. Shuangye Chen; Leader: Yiting Wang

Role: wrote the investigation paper; designed the system of curriculum(body-mind-soul-socialization) and teacher development; writing the case study.

Summary: The extreme situation of “being ill” made me realize the long-term normalization and naturalization in my original understanding of education. Reflecting on the competitive concept of competence and rethinking the sense of “living” and “capability” . It provides us an opportunity to reimagine and practice ideal education.

[5] Beautiful Promise: Narrative Inquiry on Volunteering Teachers’ Experience in Rural| Co-PI (2023)

Funded by Faculty of Education, East China Normal University. Evaluated as Outstanding.

Reflection: I think rural education is like the boulder that Sisyphus must push. I see different Sisyphus (the volunteer teachers) with complex mindset and actions. It also sheds light on the extreme situations of rural students and the institutional structures and cultural systems they are situated in.
